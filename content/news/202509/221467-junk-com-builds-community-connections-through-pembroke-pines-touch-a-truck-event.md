<p>The City of Pembroke Pines' Touch-a-Truck Vehicle Expo served as a platform for Junk.com to connect with families through hands-on vehicle interactions, with the company's truck emerging as a popular attraction among young attendees. Children lined up for opportunities to sit in the driver's seat of the Junk.com vehicle, operating the horn and posing for photographs while parents captured memorable moments. The company distributed its signature squishy truck toys, which quickly became one of the event's most sought-after giveaways.</p><p>Nicole Turturici, CEO of Junk.com, explained that while their trucks typically handle clutter removal, their participation aimed to generate excitement and curiosity among children.
