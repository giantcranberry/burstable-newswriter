This strategic move follows the path of other Chinese brands such as BYD and Zeekr, which have already established their presence in the market.</p><p>As XPeng prepares its official launch, the presence of Chinese EV makers in South Korea is set to grow stronger, potentially reshaping the balance of power in one of Asia's most competitive automotive markets. The expansion strategy of Chinese EV companies, characterized by lower prices, diverse product lines, and aggressive global approaches, could significantly impact South Korea's domestic automotive industry. Industry observers note that it would not be surprising if other Chinese EV companies like NIO Inc. also enter the South Korean market following this trend.</p><p>The entry of XPeng into South Korea represents another milestone in the global expansion of Chinese electric vehicle manufacturers.
