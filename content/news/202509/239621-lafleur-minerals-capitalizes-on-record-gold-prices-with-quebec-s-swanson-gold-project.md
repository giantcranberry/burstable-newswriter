<p>LaFleur Minerals Inc. stands to gain from current record gold prices through development of its flagship Swanson Gold Project in Quebec. The company's strategic positioning comes as unprecedented gold prices create significant opportunities for near-term producers, with the Swanson project hosting a mineral resource estimate of 123,400 ounces of gold in the indicated category and 64,500 ounces in the inferred category.</p><p>The project's location near Abitibi's prolific gold operations provides distinct advantages for rapid development. Spanning more than 18,300 hectares with a mining lease and 445 claims, the Swanson Gold Project benefits from multiple gold-bearing regional structures and district-scale consolidation. Recent consolidation efforts have created a large land package along a major structural break that hosts the Swanson, Bartec, and Jolin gold deposits, along with several other mineral showings that collectively form the Swanson Gold Project.</p><p>Infrastructure represents a critical advantage for LaFleur Minerals, with road access providing direct connection to several nearby gold mills. This accessibility significantly enhances the project's development potential and reduces capital requirements. The company's fully-permitted and refurbished Beacon Gold Mill serves as another strategic asset, capable of processing over 750 tonnes per day. This facility is being considered for processing mineralized material from the Swanson project and could potentially serve custom milling operations for other nearby gold projects.</p><p>The project's potential extends substantially beyond current resource estimates, with geological indications suggesting the possibility of expanding beyond one million ounces of gold. The consolidation of previously held properties from Monarch Mining, Abcourt Mines, and Globex Mining has created a substantial land position in one of Canada's most productive gold regions.
