<p>Datavault AI, a leader in AI data experience, valuation, and monetization, announced that IBM has made a multi-million dollar commitment of resources to support the company's platform development and go-to-market growth. The commitment includes engineering, technical sales, and quantum computing expertise from IBM, representing a significant expansion of the collaboration between the two technology companies.</p><p>The expanded partnership is expected to accelerate integration of IBM's watsonx.ai and watsonx.governance capabilities into Datavault's platform, enhance the company's product roadmap, and drive enterprise adoption worldwide. Biz Dziarmaga, Head of Americas AI Partnerships at IBM, stated that bringing IBM's resources to support Datavault's mission will help drive data monetization opportunities that companies of all sizes can leverage.</p><p>Datavault AI operates through two main divisions: the Acoustic Science Division and the Data Science Division. The Acoustic Science Division features patented technologies including WiSA, ADIO and Sumerian foundational spatial and multichannel wireless HD sound transmission technologies with intellectual property covering audio timing, synchronization and multi-channel interference cancellation. The Data Science Division leverages Web 3.0 and high-performance computing to provide solutions for experiential data perception, valuation and secure monetization.</p><p>The company's cloud-based platform serves multiple industries through HPC software licensing for sectors including sports & entertainment, events & venues, biotech, education, fintech, real estate, healthcare, and energy. A key component of Datavault's technology is the Information Data Exchange, which enables Digital Twins and licensing of name, image, and likeness by securely attaching physical real-world objects to immutable metadata objects, fostering responsible AI with integrity.</p><p>Datavault AI's technology suite offers comprehensive customization options including AI and Machine Learning automation, third-party integration, detailed analytics and data, marketing automation and advertising monitoring. The company maintains its headquarters in Beaverton, Oregon, and provides additional information through its corporate website at <a href="https://www.datavaultsite.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.datavaultsite.com</a>.</p><p>This strategic resource commitment from IBM represents a significant validation of Datavault AI's approach to data monetization and AI governance in the evolving Web 3.0 environment. The collaboration positions both companies to address growing enterprise demand for secure, responsible AI implementation and data valuation solutions across multiple industry sectors.</p>
