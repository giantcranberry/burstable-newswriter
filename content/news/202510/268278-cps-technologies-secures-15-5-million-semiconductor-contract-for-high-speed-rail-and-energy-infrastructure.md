<p>CPS Technologies Corp. (NASDAQ: CPSH) has finalized a $15.5 million contract with a multinational semiconductor manufacturer, marking a significant expansion in their business relationship. The agreement, which commenced October 1, 2025, involves the delivery of advanced power module components over a 12-month period and represents a 16.5% year-over-year increase in business from this customer.</p><p>The components specified in the contract will be integrated into systems supporting high-speed rail transportation as well as energy and grid infrastructure applications. This expanded engagement highlights the growing demand for CPS' aluminum silicon carbide product line across multiple industrial sectors. President and CEO Brian Mackey stated that the company is encouraged by the continued momentum in this product category and sees rising demand across industrial, energy, and mobility markets.</p><p>Mackey emphasized that CPS remains focused on delivering performance-driven solutions that help customers meet growing needs, particularly in electrification, transportation, and energy applications. The contract reflects the strength of customer relationships and CPS' expanding role in powering next-generation technologies. According to Mackey, such orders validate the company's plan to relocate to a larger and more efficient production facility, which was enabled by their recent capital raise.</p><p>CPS Technologies specializes in high-performance materials solutions for various applications, including electric trains and subway cars, wind turbines, hybrid and electric vehicles, Navy ships, the smart electric grid, and 5G infrastructure. The company's products also include hermetic packages for aerospace and satellite applications and lightweight armor products providing ballistic protection and environmental durability. The company articulates its vision as pioneering the next generation of high-performance materials to solve the world's toughest engineering challenges while supporting the transition to clean energy. Additional information about CPS Technologies can be found at <a href="https://www.ctc.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.ctc.com</a>.</p><p>This contract expansion signals broader industry trends toward electrification and advanced power management solutions. For business leaders and technology executives, the growing demand for CPS' components reflects the accelerating global transition to clean energy infrastructure and advanced transportation systems. The 16.5% year-over-year increase demonstrates sustained momentum in industrial electrification markets, suggesting continued investment opportunities in companies supporting these technological transitions. The integration of these components into high-speed rail and energy grid applications underscores the critical role advanced materials play in modernizing infrastructure and achieving sustainability goals across multiple sectors.</p>
