<p>NW Legacy Law has promoted Jakob Seegmuller to the position of managing attorney, marking a significant leadership transition for the Vancouver-based legal firm. Seegmuller, who joined the practice in 2018, has built his reputation serving clients across Washington and Oregon with specialized focus on estate planning, probate, and trust administration.</p><p>In his new leadership capacity, Seegmuller will support NW Legacy Law's mission of serving the Clark County community by providing clear, thoughtful, and confident legal guidance. His approach has been characterized by both compassion and practicality, offering clients thoughtful guidance to achieve their estate planning goals while navigating the complexities that follow the loss of a loved one. The firm's founder, Thomas Hackett, expressed confidence in Seegmuller's leadership capabilities, stating his dedication to clarity and compassion makes him the ideal person to guide the team forward.</p><p>NW Legacy Law was founded over ten years ago by Thomas Hackett and one paralegal, growing from humble beginnings to become an established legal practice. The firm has expanded to include four attorneys and a dedicated team of compassionate paralegals, client service specialists, and support staff. Based in Vancouver's historic Officers Row, NW Legacy Law proudly serves clients as trusted estate planning lawyers in WA through their website at <a href="https://www.nwlegacylaw.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.nwlegacylaw.com</a>.</p><p>The promotion represents the firm's commitment to maintaining its core values while adapting to meet evolving client needs in the Pacific Northwest legal landscape. Seegmuller's elevation to managing attorney signals continuity in the firm's approach to estate planning services while bringing fresh leadership perspective to the growing practice. This leadership transition demonstrates how established legal firms are strategically positioning themselves for future growth while preserving the client-focused values that built their reputation.</p><p>For business leaders observing the professional services sector, this promotion illustrates the importance of internal talent development and succession planning. The seamless transition from founder-led to next-generation leadership provides a model for other small to mid-sized professional firms navigating similar growth phases. The firm's expansion from a single attorney and paralegal to a comprehensive team of legal professionals reflects broader trends in the legal industry toward specialized, client-centered practices that prioritize both technical expertise and emotional intelligence in serving clients during sensitive life transitions.</p>
