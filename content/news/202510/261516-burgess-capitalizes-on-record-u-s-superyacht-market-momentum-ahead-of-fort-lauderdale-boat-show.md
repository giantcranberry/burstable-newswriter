<p>The American superyacht market continues to demonstrate record-breaking strength as Burgess, the global leader in luxury yachting, prepares for the 2025 Fort Lauderdale International Boat Show with strong momentum following three major consecutive sales. The company's recent success underscores the vitality of U.S. demand and reinforces Burgess' growing presence across the Americas. This sustained performance signals robust health in the high-end luxury sector despite broader economic uncertainties, suggesting American wealth remains actively invested in premium experiences and assets.</p><p>Jonathan Beckett, CEO of Burgess, noted the rising confidence in the U.S. luxury market, stating that American clients are showing unprecedented enthusiasm for both new builds and brokerage opportunities. The Fort Lauderdale International Boat Show remains one of the world's premier stages for yachting, and Burgess looks forward to continuing that momentum with a standout fleet and energized team. With five decades of industry leadership and 18 offices worldwide, Burgess continues to expand its U.S. operations, leveraging a surge in American ownership and charter activity. This expansion reflects strategic positioning to capture growing market share in the world's largest luxury consumer market.</p><p>At this year's show, the firm will present a dynamic lineup of yachts reflecting the depth of today's market, from cutting-edge catamarans to classic Northern European builds, each representing the Burgess standard of excellence in design, performance, and pedigree. The featured yachts include the 60-meter SCOTT FREE from Abeking & Rasmussen, Germany, available for EUR 43,500,000 with charter rates from USD 420,000 per week. This exemplary build combines timeless Starkey exterior lines with a bold interior by Bannenberg & Rowell, featuring vibrant textures and a sculptural spiral staircase. The vessel underwent a major refit in 2022 and completed her 15-year class survey in 2024. Interested parties can view the full details through the <a href="https://www.burgessyachts.com/en/sales/charter/scott-free" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Online Listing</a>.</p><p>HEMISPHERE, the world's largest sailing catamaran at 44.2 meters from Pendennis Shipyard in the United Kingdom, is priced at EUR 46,000,000 with charter rates from USD 260,000 per week. Her impressive 16.6-meter beam creates deck spaces rivaling those of a 70-meter yacht, offering versatile spaces for relaxation and entertainment. The award-winning interiors blend refinement with warmth, featuring two VIP cabins on the main deck that open onto a private terrace lounge.
