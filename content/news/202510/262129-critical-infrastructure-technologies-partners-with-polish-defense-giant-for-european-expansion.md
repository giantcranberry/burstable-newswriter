<p>Critical Infrastructure Technologies Ltd. has established a formal collaboration framework with H. Cegielski - Poznań S.A., a subsidiary of Polska Grupa Zbrojeniowa S.A., through a recently executed Non-Disclosure Agreement. This partnership enables secure exchange of confidential information related to manufacturing CiTech's Nexus Tower system, marking a significant step in the company's European industrial expansion strategy.</p><p>The agreement facilitates protected sharing of technical documentation, design data, and proprietary materials associated with production and assembly of the Nexus system. HCP brings substantial expertise in heavy manufacturing, precision engineering, and defense-sector production capabilities, making the company an optimal partner for advancing the Nexus Tower program. This collaboration leverages HCP's industrial facilities and workforce while accessing the extensive defense manufacturing infrastructure of the PGZ Group.</p><p>Brenton Scott, CEO of CiTech, characterized the NDA signing as a key milestone in the company's international growth strategy. He noted that HCP's world-class manufacturing capability, supported by PGZ's resources, aligns with CiTech's objective of delivering advanced, relocatable infrastructure platforms for defense, emergency response, and critical communications applications.</p><p>The Nexus Tower Project involves design and production of a 20-meter autonomous, relocatable tower system capable of rapid deployment to support communications, surveillance, and integrated mission systems. This technology addresses limitations in current rapidly deployable communication solutions through patented self-deploying platform technology designed to support radio equipment including LTE and various technological payloads such as surveillance and anti-drone systems. For additional information about the company and its technologies, visit <a href="https://www.citech.com.au" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.citech.com.au</a>.</p><p>This partnership positions CiTech to better serve defense, border security, surveillance, and emergency services sectors across Europe through efficient production scaling. The collaboration represents a strategic alignment between innovative infrastructure technology and established defense manufacturing expertise, potentially enhancing Europe's capabilities in rapid-response communications and surveillance infrastructure for both military and civilian emergency applications.</p>
