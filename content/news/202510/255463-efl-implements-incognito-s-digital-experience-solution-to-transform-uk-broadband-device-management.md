<p>Electronic Frontier Ltd (EFL), a prominent value-added distributor serving the UK telecommunications market, has implemented Incognito Software Systems' Digital Experience (DX) Solution to deliver enhanced device management capabilities to its broadband provider customers. The partnership, facilitated through value-added partner BM COM, represents a significant advancement in how UK service providers manage their broadband infrastructure.</p><p>The cloud-based platform will provide EFL's customers with centralized visibility and control over broadband devices, enabling faster service activation processes and more efficient operational management. Service providers will benefit from proactive Wi-Fi management capabilities and improved troubleshooting tools, which are expected to contribute to reduced operational costs across their networks.</p><p>The technical foundation of the solution includes an award-winning TR-069 Auto Configuration Server (ACS) with TR-369 User Services Platform (USP) support, ensuring broad interoperability across diverse device ecosystems. This dual-standard approach allows for compatibility with existing TR-069 technologies while providing forward-looking support for next-generation standards, futureproofing EFL's service offerings to multiple service provider clients.</p><p>Mark Torrans, VP of EMEA Sales at BM COM, emphasized the strategic importance of the partnership, noting that working with established distributors like EFL has been a key element of their growth strategy in the UK telecom market. The collaboration builds on two decades of previous work between the organizations and leverages BM COM's cloud expertise to deliver the managed platform solution.</p><p>Conor McCann, General Manager of EFL, highlighted the comprehensive nature of the device management tools provided by Incognito, describing them as essential for effective operation in today's competitive broadband market. The solution addresses critical needs for service providers seeking to optimize their device management processes while maintaining service quality and customer satisfaction.</p><p>The implementation represents a significant step in the evolution of broadband service management in the UK, where efficient device management has become increasingly crucial for service providers competing in a rapidly evolving market. By leveraging Incognito's carrier-grade solution through the cloud delivery model managed by BM COM, EFL positions itself to offer enhanced value to its telecommunications customers while supporting the industry's transition toward more advanced broadband management standards. Additional information about Incognito Software Systems can be found at <a href="https://www.incognito.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.incognito.com</a>.</p>
