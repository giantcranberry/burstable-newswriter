<p>SKYX Platforms Corp., operating as SKYX Technologies, has entered into a strategic agreement with Chicago-based Global Ventures Group to deploy its advanced smart home and building technologies across residential, commercial, and hotel projects throughout the Middle East. The partnership specifically targets developments in Saudi Arabia and Egypt, marking a substantial expansion of SKYX's global footprint and positioning the company at the forefront of smart infrastructure development in rapidly growing markets.</p><p>The collaboration aligns with SKYX's broader global expansion strategy and its mission to establish smarter, safer, and more connected homes and buildings as the new standard. Under the agreement, SKYX anticipates supplying hundreds of thousands of units of its plug-and-play smart products, including ceiling lighting, fans, recessed and wall lights, EXIT and emergency lights, and LED mirrors. Global Ventures Group, led by Founder and CEO Randall Langer, will integrate these technologies to enhance safety, convenience, and design standards throughout the region.</p><p>This expansion into Middle Eastern markets represents a strategic move to capitalize on the region's significant infrastructure development and smart city initiatives. The deployment of SKYX's technologies across hundreds of thousands of units signals both market demand and the scalability of smart building solutions in emerging economies. For business leaders and technology executives, this partnership demonstrates the growing global appetite for integrated smart systems that prioritize both safety and user experience.</p><p>SKYX Platforms emphasizes that its technologies represent a necessity for every room in homes and buildings globally, positioning electricity as a standard that should be complemented by advanced safety and smart features. The company maintains a portfolio of highly disruptive platform technologies supported by over 97 U.S. and global patents and patent pending applications. These technologies prioritize high quality and ease of use while significantly enhancing both safety and lifestyle in residential and commercial spaces.</p><p>The implications for the broader smart home and building technology industry are substantial, as this partnership establishes a blueprint for international expansion and technology adoption in developing markets.
