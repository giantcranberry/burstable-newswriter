<p>Friend Commercial Real Estate recently facilitated the sale of a two-story retail building in Historic Downtown Annapolis, located just steps from City Dock. Lynn Dulin, CCIM of Friend Commercial Real Estate represented the Seller in the transaction, which involved a local investor as the Buyer and quietly closed this past fall.</p><p>The building, totaling approximately 1,956 square feet, had been in the same family for decades, with this transaction marking the first arms-length sale of the property since the late 1960s. The property is currently leased to a local clothing boutique, maintaining its commercial presence in the downtown area. This continuity in use represents stability for the local business community while the ownership transitions to new hands.</p><p>This sale represented the end of a long chapter for a family with deep ties to the Annapolis community, said Dulin. We're proud to have helped transition this Main Street gem to new local ownership, where it will continue to contribute to the area's vibrant mix of retail, dining, and cultural experiences. The transaction demonstrates the ongoing appeal of historic downtown properties to local investors who understand the value of preserving the character of Annapolis while supporting its commercial vitality.</p><p>The sale highlights several important trends in the Annapolis real estate market, including the strong demand for well-located historic properties and the preference for local ownership that understands the community's unique character. Properties in the Historic Downtown district continue to attract significant interest due to their prime location near City Dock and the enduring appeal of Main Street retail spaces. The successful transaction also underscores the importance of experienced commercial real estate representation in navigating complex property transfers involving historic assets.</p><p>Friend Commercial Real Estate maintains its position as a leading provider of commercial real estate services throughout the region, with additional information available at <a href="https://www.friendre.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.friendre.com</a>. The firm's expertise in representing both sellers and buyers in transactions involving historic properties contributes to the preservation of Annapolis's architectural heritage while facilitating responsible commercial development.</p>
