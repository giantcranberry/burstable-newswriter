<p>The recent opening of a Once Upon A Child store in Wilson represents a significant development in both local retail economics and sustainable business practices. Located at 2101 Tarboro St., the store offers gently used children's clothing, shoes, toys, accessories, and equipment at reduced prices, addressing both financial and environmental concerns for area families.</p><p>Local owner Rease Newton, a mother of three and former educator, identified the need for such a business after regularly making 45-minute trips to the nearest location. Wilson is such a hardworking, family-oriented community, and I'm excited to give local parents a place where they can save money, shop sustainably, and connect with others, Newton stated. She operates the business with her parents, Tom and Tonya Allen, who serve as co-owners.</p><p>The store's business model differs significantly from traditional consignment operations by offering immediate payment for items without requiring appointments. This approach enables families to quickly convert their gently used children's items into cash while providing affordable shopping options for other parents. The economic implications for Wilson families are substantial, particularly given current inflationary pressures affecting household budgets.</p><p>From a sustainability perspective, the store's operations align with circular economy principles that are gaining traction across the retail industry. By facilitating the reuse of children's items, the business helps reduce textile and plastic waste while making quality products accessible to more families in the community, creating both immediate economic benefits and long-term environmental advantages.</p>
