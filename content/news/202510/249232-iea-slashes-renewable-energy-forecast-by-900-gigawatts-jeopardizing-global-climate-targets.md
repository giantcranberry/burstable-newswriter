<p>The International Energy Agency has substantially downgraded its global renewable energy capacity projections for 2030, cutting nearly 900 gigawatts from previous forecasts due to policy reversals in major economies including the United States and China. The Paris-based organization now anticipates 4,600 gigawatts of renewable capacity by the end of the decade, down from last year's projection of 5,500 gigawatts. This revision effectively confirms that the international target of tripling clean energy deployment by 2030 will not be achieved.</p><p>The policy changes in China and the United States represent significant setbacks for global climate efforts, as these two nations are among the world's largest energy consumers and carbon emitters.
