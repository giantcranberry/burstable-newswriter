<p>HeartBeam has announced a strategic partnership with HeartNexus Inc. to deliver comprehensive remote cardiology services, enabling 24/7 access to cardiac care interpretation for patients experiencing symptoms outside traditional medical facilities. This collaboration represents a significant advancement in making specialized cardiac care more accessible through technological innovation, potentially transforming how cardiac emergencies are managed in non-clinical settings.</p><p>The partnership leverages HeartBeam's patented 3D ECG technology, which allows patients to capture 30-second, cable-free electrocardiogram recordings whenever cardiac symptoms occur. These recordings are immediately transmitted to HeartNexus cardiologists for professional interpretation, providing real-time feedback that could be critical for timely medical intervention. Robert Eno, CEO of HeartBeam, emphasized that working with HeartNexus accelerates the company's mission to make medical-grade cardiac monitoring accessible beyond conventional healthcare settings.</p><p>HeartNexus brings specialized expertise to the partnership as a network of board-certified cardiologists focusing on cardiac test interpretation, peer consultations, and telemedicine services. The integration of their clinical expertise with HeartBeam's technological platform creates a comprehensive ecosystem for remote cardiac care. This collaboration not only provides immediate cardiology reader services but also lays the foundation for future integration of AI wellness features and wearable technology enhancements, positioning the partnership at the forefront of digital health innovation.</p><p>The technological foundation of this partnership rests on HeartBeam's FDA-cleared 3D ECG platform, which received clearance for arrhythmia assessment in December 2024. The company's innovative approach involves creating the first cable-free device capable of collecting ECG signals from three non-coplanar directions and synthesizing them into a 12-lead ECG format. This technology, protected by over 20 issued patents, enables physicians to identify cardiac health trends and acute conditions while directing patients to appropriate care outside traditional medical facilities.
