<p>Safe & Green Holdings Corp. has successfully regained compliance with all Nasdaq listing requirements, marking a significant milestone for the modular construction company. Nasdaq confirmed the matter was closed as of October 3, 2025, following the company's resolution of issues related to Listing Rule 5550(a)(2) concerning the minimum $1.00 bid price requirement.</p><p>The compliance achievement follows a comprehensive restructuring plan that included renegotiating the company's agreement with Boral, resulting in an 80% reduction in potential dilution. The restructuring also eliminated Ace warrants and resolved Nasdaq's prior public interest concerns regarding potential share issuance. A subsequent reverse stock split further reduced dilution by an additional 64%, strengthening the company's capital structure significantly.</p><p>Chief Executive Officer Mike McLaren emphasized that this milestone demonstrates Safe & Green's commitment to fortifying its financial foundation while advancing future growth initiatives. The company's successful compliance restoration comes at a critical time for the modular construction industry, which continues to see increased demand for sustainable infrastructure solutions across multiple sectors.</p><p>Safe & Green Holdings operates as a leading provider of modular construction and sustainable infrastructure solutions, serving customers across healthcare, education, energy, and government sectors. The company's subsidiaries focus on delivering innovative, cost-efficient, and environmentally conscious solutions that drive long-term value creation. Additional information about the company's operations and services can be found at <a href="https://www.safeandgreenholdings.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.safeandgreenholdings.com/</a>.</p><p>The Nasdaq compliance restoration represents more than just regulatory achievement for Safe & Green Holdings. It signals improved investor confidence and market stability for a company operating in the rapidly evolving modular construction space. The capital structure enhancements position the company to better capitalize on growing demand for sustainable building solutions while maintaining its Nasdaq listing status, which provides continued access to public markets and enhanced visibility among investors.</p><p>This development comes as the modular construction industry continues to gain traction globally, driven by factors including construction efficiency, sustainability concerns, and the need for rapid deployment of infrastructure projects. Safe & Green's strengthened financial position enables the company to pursue growth opportunities more effectively while maintaining compliance with exchange requirements that ensure ongoing market access and transparency for shareholders.</p>
