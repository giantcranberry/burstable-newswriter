<p>Silvercorp Metals Inc. (TSX: SVM) (NYSE American: SVM) has unveiled its financial results for the first quarter of Fiscal 2026, showcasing a net income of $18.1 million, or $0.08 per share, on revenue of $81.3 million. This revenue was primarily driven by the sale of 1.8 million ounces of silver, alongside significant quantities of gold, lead, and zinc, reflecting the company's strong operational performance and market demand for precious and base metals.</p><p>The company's production metrics were equally notable, with 1.8 million ounces of silver, 2,050 ounces of gold, 15.7 million pounds of lead, and 5.2 million pounds of zinc produced. These figures translate to a total of 2.0 million ounces of silver equivalent, underscoring Silvercorp's efficiency and capacity in mining operations. The all-in sustaining cost was reported at $13.49 per ounce of silver, net of by-product credits, indicating cost-effective production strategies.</p><p>Adjusted net income and EBITDA attributable to equity shareholders were reported at $21.0 million and $33.8 million, respectively, with operating cash flow reaching $48.3 million. This robust financial performance contributed to $22.5 million in free cash flow, reinforcing the company's financial stability and ability to fund future growth initiatives.</p><p>Strategic investments during the quarter included $18.8 million in China operations and $5.4 million in Ecuador operations, alongside $2.7 million paid in dividends. Silvercorp's strong financial position is further highlighted by $377.1 million in cash and equivalents and $72.2 million in equity investments. A notable development was the $175 million stream financing commitment from Wheaton Precious Metals International Ltd. for the El Domo project, signaling confidence in Silvercorp's growth prospects and operational capabilities.</p><p>For more insights into Silvercorp's financial performance and strategic direction, visit <a href='https://ibn.fm/rubWN' rel='nofollow' target='_blank'>https://ibn.fm/rubWN</a>.
