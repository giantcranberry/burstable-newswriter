LOCOAL, Das GreenHaus, and Toss It Dumpsters Forge Path to Sustainability in Texas Hill Country

Building Texas Show News • June 18, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

This week, Kerrville hosts Venture Fest 2024, featuring LOCOAL, Das GreenHaus, and Toss It Dumpsters. The event showcases innovative business collaborations aiming to revolutionize decarbonization technology and sustainable development.

LOCOAL partners with Das GreenHaus to bring next-gen decarbonization solutions to the Texas Hill Country. The collaboration includes a shared headquarters facility with Toss It Dumpsters, focusing on waste-to-energy systems and regional sustainability initiatives. LOCOAL's flagship technology, RAINMAKER™, is at the forefront of transforming organic waste into renewable resources, with a vision to scale globally.

Full Article

The Texas Hill Country is set to become a beacon of sustainability and innovation thanks to a groundbreaking partnership between LOCOAL, Das GreenHaus (DGH), and Toss It Dumpsters. This collaboration, announced during Venture Fest 2024 in Kerrville, represents a significant leap forward in the region's efforts to combat climate change while fostering economic development. LOCOAL's RAINMAKER™ technology, which transforms organic waste into renewable resources, is at the heart of this initiative, promising to revolutionize waste management and energy production in the area.

The partnership underscores the importance of local collaboration in addressing global environmental challenges. By co-locating at a new headquarters in the Hill Country, LOCOAL and Toss It Dumpsters will leverage their combined expertise to create a circular economy for waste, turning materials like construction wood and cedar trees into biochar and clean energy. This approach not only mitigates landfill use and carbon emissions but also sets a precedent for sustainable business practices that could inspire similar efforts worldwide.

Justin McKenzie of Das GreenHaus highlighted the partnership's alignment with the nonprofit's mission to connect innovators and community leaders for the betterment of the Hill Country. Meanwhile, Walt McNeil of Toss It Dumpsters expressed excitement about the potential of LOCOAL's technology to transform waste management. Miles Murray, LOCOAL's CEO, shared his vision for the project to catalyze a new era of innovation in the region, rooted in the principles of environmental stewardship and economic growth.

This initiative is more than just a local development; it's a model for sustainable living that could have far-reaching implications. By demonstrating the viability of integrating advanced recycling technologies with traditional waste management practices, the partnership offers a blueprint for reducing carbon footprints and promoting renewable energy sources. As the project progresses, it may attract significant interest from investors and environmental advocates, positioning the Texas Hill Country as a leader in green technology and sustainable development.

The collaboration between LOCOAL, Das GreenHaus, and Toss It Dumpsters is a testament to the power of innovation and community in tackling some of the most pressing environmental issues of our time. With its potential to reduce waste, lower carbon emissions, and stimulate economic growth, this partnership could serve as a catalyst for change, not just in Texas but around the world.
