Rare 1992 Ferrari 512 TR Finds New Home at DFW Car & Toy Museum

Building Texas Show News • June 18, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The DFW Car & Toy Museum unveils the iconic 1992 Ferrari 512 TR as part of the Ron Sturgeon Collection, showcasing a rare example of Ferrari's 12-cylinder excellence. This well-preserved supercar is a testament to Italian automotive glory from the '90s, featuring original accessories and a classic red exterior with tan interior.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum, a beacon for automotive enthusiasts and historians alike, has recently welcomed a significant addition to its prestigious collection. A 1992 Ferrari 512 TR, part of the Ron Sturgeon Collection, now graces the museum, offering visitors a glimpse into a pivotal era of automotive design and engineering. This vehicle, with its fiery red exterior and limited production numbers, stands as a rare jewel among Ferrari's 12-cylinder masterpieces.

With only about 400 units officially imported into the United States during its production run, the 512 TR is a testament to the exclusivity and high performance that defined Ferrari in the early 1990s. The car's inclusion in the museum not only enriches the collection but also serves as an educational tool, illustrating the evolution of automotive technology and design. Its pristine condition, complete with original documentation and accessories, provides an unparalleled opportunity for enthusiasts to connect with automotive history.

The DFW Car & Toy Museum, founded by Ron Sturgeon, is renowned for its extensive collection of rare and significant automobiles. The addition of the 1992 Ferrari 512 TR underscores the museum's commitment to preserving automotive heritage. Visitors can explore this and over 200 other exceptional vehicles, all under one roof, free of charge. This initiative not only fosters a deeper appreciation for automotive history but also highlights the economic and cultural impact of such collections in Texas.

The 512 TR, an evolution of the iconic Testarossa, represents the zenith of 1990s automotive aesthetics and engineering. Its presence in the museum offers a tangible link to an era of unparalleled Italian craftsmanship. For car enthusiasts, historians, and the general public, the DFW Car & Toy Museum's latest acquisition is a reminder of the enduring allure and significance of automotive history.
