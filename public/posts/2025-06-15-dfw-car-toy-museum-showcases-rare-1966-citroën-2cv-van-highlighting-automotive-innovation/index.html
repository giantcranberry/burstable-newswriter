DFW Car & Toy Museum Showcases Rare 1966 Citroën 2CV Van, Highlighting Automotive Innovation

Building Texas Show News • June 15, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The DFW Car & Toy Museum in Fort Worth, TX, is showcasing a rare 1966 Citroën 2CV van from the Ron Sturgeon Collection, highlighting its historical significance and quirky design. Visitors can admire this iconic vehicle known for its practicality and charm, offering a unique glimpse into automotive history.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum has recently added a remarkable piece to its collection, a 1966 Citroën 2CV van, known as the Fourgonnette, from the esteemed Ron Sturgeon Collection. This vehicle is not just a classic car; it's a testament to an era where automotive design prioritized functionality and innovation over speed and luxury. The Citroën 2CV van, with its distinctive two-cylinder engine and four-speed manual transmission, served as a reliable workhorse for over four decades, embodying the practical needs of its time.

Ron Sturgeon, the museum's owner, highlights the rarity of such a well-preserved 2CV van in the United States, making its display a unique opportunity for visitors. The vehicle's inclusion in the museum's collection allows both automotive aficionados and the general public to delve into a significant chapter of transportation history. The 2CV van's design reflects a period when creativity in automotive engineering sought to meet the everyday needs of people, marking a departure from the conventional focus on performance and aesthetics.

Located in North Fort Worth, the DFW Car & Toy Museum offers a wide-ranging look at automotive history through its assortment of classic cars, toys, and memorabilia. The addition of the Citroën 2CV van enriches this experience, providing insights into the evolution of vehicle design and its impact on society. The museum, open Tuesday through Saturday with free admission and parking, invites visitors to explore this and other exhibits, offering a tangible connection to the past and inspiring appreciation for the ingenuity of automotive pioneers.

This exhibit not only celebrates the Citroën 2CV van's historical significance but also serves as a reminder of how far automotive technology and design have come. It underscores the importance of preserving such artifacts to educate future generations about the innovations that have shaped our world. The DFW Car & Toy Museum's commitment to showcasing these treasures plays a crucial role in keeping the legacy of automotive history alive for enthusiasts and historians alike.
