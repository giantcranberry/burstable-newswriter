Courageous Conservatives PAC Fights Back Against SLAPP Lawsuit, Defending Free Speech in Texas

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The Courageous Conservatives Political Action Committee is set to challenge a lawsuit filed by Rep. Pat Curry, which they label as a 'SLAPP' lawsuit aimed at silencing critics and suppressing free speech. The PAC, led by Chairman Chris Ekstrom, argues that the lawsuit is a politically motivated attack on grassroots conservatives and their right to free speech. The case revolves around communications that urged public engagement on the election of the Texas House Speaker, which the PAC asserts are protected under the Texas Citizens Participation Act. The PAC is not only seeking to dismiss the lawsuit but also pursuing attorney’s fees and sanctions against what they deem a frivolous and retaliatory legal action.

Chris Ekstrom emphasized the importance of defending free speech and political accountability, stating that the lawsuit sets a dangerous precedent for public officials to sue citizens and organizations for civic engagement. The hearing is scheduled for June 6, 2025, in Waco, Texas, where the PAC will present its case against Curry's lawsuit. The Courageous Conservatives PAC remains committed to its mission of electing principled conservative leaders and ensuring the voices of patriotic Americans are heard.

Full Article

The Courageous Conservatives Political Action Committee (PAC) is currently embroiled in a legal battle that underscores the delicate balance between free speech and political accountability in Texas. At the heart of the dispute is a lawsuit filed by Rep. Pat Curry, which the PAC alleges is a strategic lawsuit against public participation (SLAPP), designed to intimidate and silence its critics. The PAC, a staunch advocate for conservative values, is leveraging the Texas Citizens Participation Act to seek dismissal of the lawsuit, arguing that the communications in question—texts and Facebook posts—were lawful efforts to foster civic engagement.

Chris Ekstrom, Chairman of the Courageous Conservatives PAC, has been vocal in his criticism of the lawsuit, framing it as a politically motivated attack on the fundamental rights of free speech and petition. The PAC's legal team, led by Jerad Najvar, contends that Rep. Curry's lawsuit fails to address the legal merits of the case, particularly the protections afforded to political speech under the TCPA. The PAC's stance is not merely defensive; it is also seeking attorney’s fees and sanctions against what it views as a frivolous and retaliatory legal action.

The implications of this legal confrontation extend far beyond the immediate parties involved. A hearing scheduled for June 6, 2025, in Waco, TX, could set a precedent for how similar cases are handled in the future, potentially influencing the landscape of political discourse and accountability in Texas. The outcome may also serve as a litmus test for the robustness of First Amendment protections in the face of legal challenges from public officials. For businesses and organizations operating in Texas, the case highlights the importance of understanding the legal protections available for political speech and the potential risks of engaging in public debate.

This case is a reminder of the critical role that free speech plays in a functioning democracy, especially in the context of political engagement and accountability. The Courageous Conservatives PAC's fight against what it perceives as an attempt to stifle dissent is not just about the specifics of this lawsuit; it's about defending the principles that underpin the American Republic. As the legal proceedings unfold, the eyes of Texas—and indeed, the nation—will be watching closely to see how the courts navigate the complex interplay between law, politics, and free speech.
