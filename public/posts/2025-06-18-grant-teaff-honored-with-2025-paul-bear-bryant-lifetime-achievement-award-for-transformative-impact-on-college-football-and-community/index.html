Grant Teaff Honored with 2025 Paul 'Bear' Bryant Lifetime Achievement Award for Transformative Impact on College Football and Community

Building Texas Show News • June 18, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Grant Teaff, former Baylor University head football coach and executive director of the American Football Coaches Association, is set to receive the American Heart Association's prestigious 2025 Paul “Bear” Bryant Lifetime Achievement Award. The award, sponsored by Memorial Hermann Health System, recognizes outstanding contributions to college football and will be presented at the Paul “Bear” Bryant Coach of the Year Awards ceremony in Houston. Teaff's coaching legacy and philanthropic work make him a deserving recipient of this honor.

Full Article

The announcement of Grant Teaff as the recipient of the 2025 Paul 'Bear' Bryant Lifetime Achievement Award by the American Heart Association marks a significant moment in the recognition of individuals who have profoundly impacted college football and beyond. Teaff's illustrious career, notably his transformation of Baylor University's football program and his leadership at the American Football Coaches Association (AFCA), underscores the award's criteria of excellence, integrity, and community service.

Teaff's tenure at Baylor University from 1972 to 1992 is a testament to his coaching prowess, turning a struggling team into a formidable competitor within the Southwest Conference. His achievements, including two conference championships and multiple bowl game appearances, highlight his ability to inspire and lead. Beyond the field, Teaff's role in expanding the AFCA and his induction into multiple halls of fame reflect his lasting influence on college athletics.

The Lifetime Achievement Award, presented during the annual Paul 'Bear' Bryant Coach of the Year Awards ceremony in Houston, Texas, not only celebrates Teaff's coaching legacy but also supports the American Heart Association's mission. This alignment underscores the importance of heart health awareness and research, leveraging the platform of sports to address one of the leading causes of death globally.

Teaff's dedication to community service, through initiatives like Camp Success and the Grant Teaff Foundation, further exemplifies the award's spirit. His commitment to developing young athletes' character and leadership skills resonates with the values the Paul 'Bear' Bryant Awards seek to honor. As the college football community gathers to celebrate Teaff's achievements, his legacy serves as a beacon for current and future coaches, emphasizing the transformative power of sports beyond the game.
