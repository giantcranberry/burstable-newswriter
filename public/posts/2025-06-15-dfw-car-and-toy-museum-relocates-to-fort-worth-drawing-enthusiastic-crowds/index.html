DFW Car and Toy Museum Relocates to Fort Worth, Drawing Enthusiastic Crowds

Building Texas Show News • June 15, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Ron Sturgeon, a collector of toys and cars, has opened the DFW Car and Toy Museum in a new 150,000 square foot facility in Fort Worth, TX. The museum showcases over 200 cars, 3,000 toys, and offers free parking and admission. The grand opening is set for April 12, 2025, with special events planned for visitors.

Full Article

The DFW Car and Toy Museum, under the stewardship of collector Ron Sturgeon, has successfully transitioned to its new home in Fort Worth, marking a new chapter in its mission to celebrate automotive and toy history. The museum's inaugural day at its 150,000-square-foot facility on McMillan Parkway saw over 600 guests, a testament to the community's enthusiasm for Sturgeon's curated collections. This relocation not only amplifies the museum's visibility, thanks to its proximity to Interstate 35 and enhanced signage, but also significantly upgrades the visitor experience through extensive renovations.

Sturgeon's journey from toy collecting in the 1980s to amassing an impressive array of automobiles through his auto salvage business is at the heart of the museum's diverse offerings. Highlights include more than 35 Volkswagens, a nod to Sturgeon's early career, alongside a vast selection of toys. The museum's commitment to accessibility is evident in its free admission and parking policies, inviting a broad audience to explore the intersections of automotive and toy history.

The museum's calendar is already filling with notable events, such as the Antique Toy Collectors of America's annual convention and a cars and coffee gathering, anticipated to attract even larger crowds. These events, coupled with the museum's operational hours and additional services like climate-controlled car storage and event facilities, position the DFW Car and Toy Museum as a pivotal cultural and economic asset in Texas. Its dog-friendly policy and encouragement of photography further underscore its role as an interactive and inclusive destination for collectors and families alike.

This expansion reflects a growing interest in niche museums and their ability to draw tourists and locals, contributing to the local economy and fostering a sense of community among enthusiasts. The DFW Car and Toy Museum's move is not just a change of address but a significant enhancement of its capacity to educate, entertain, and inspire, making it a noteworthy development for Texas's cultural landscape.
