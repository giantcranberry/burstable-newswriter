DFW Car & Toy Museum Showcases Rare 1972 Alfa Romeo Montreal, Highlighting Italian Automotive Excellence

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Discover the rare and iconic 1972 Alfa Romeo Montreal showcased at the DFW Car & Toy Museum in Fort Worth, TX. With only 3,900 models ever built, this left-hand-drive beauty offers a glimpse into the blend of Italian design and high-performance engineering. Visitors can also explore over 200 classic and collector cars in the Ron Sturgeon Collection, making it a must-visit destination for car enthusiasts and families alike.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum, nestled in North Fort Worth, has emerged as a beacon for automotive enthusiasts and history buffs alike, thanks to its impressive collection of over 200 classic vehicles. Among these, the 1972 Alfa Romeo Montreal stands out as a crown jewel, showcasing the pinnacle of Italian automotive design and engineering. This rare vehicle, one of only 3,900 models produced between 1971 and 1975, offers a unique window into the past, presenting an opportunity to appreciate the evolution of automotive technology and design.

The 1972 Alfa Romeo Montreal is not just any classic car; it's a piece of automotive history. Originally unveiled at the 1970 Geneva Motor Show, this left-hand-drive model is powered by a fuel-injected 2.6-liter V8 engine coupled with a five-speed ZF manual transmission. Enhancements such as Alfaholics suspension components and track rod ends have been added to improve its handling, making it a marvel of engineering. Inside, the vehicle boasts vintage Italian craftsmanship, including a three-spoke wood-rimmed steering wheel and Jaeger instruments, with its factory Borletti air conditioning system still in working order—a rarity among classic Italian sports cars.

Beyond the Alfa Romeo Montreal, the museum offers free admission and a dog-friendly environment, making it an accessible destination for families and car enthusiasts. The Ron Sturgeon Collection, a highlight of the museum, represents decades of automotive passion and preservation. Open Tuesday through Saturday, the 150,000-square-foot facility not only displays these remarkable vehicles but also provides climate-controlled storage and event spaces for car clubs and organizations, further cementing its role in the automotive community.

The significance of the DFW Car & Toy Museum and its 1972 Alfa Romeo Montreal extends beyond mere nostalgia. It serves as an educational resource, offering insights into the technological advancements and design philosophies of the past. For the automotive industry, it underscores the importance of preservation and the enduring appeal of classic design. For visitors, it's an opportunity to connect with history, inspiring a new generation of car enthusiasts and preserving the legacy of automotive innovation for years to come.
