Ferrari 575M Maranello Joins DFW Car & Toy Museum's Ron Sturgeon Collection

Building Texas Show News • June 15, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Experience the power and elegance of the 2002 Ferrari 575M Maranello at the DFW Car & Toy Museum, part of the Ron Sturgeon Collection. This iconic red Ferrari boasts a 5.7-liter V12 engine, Skyhook suspension, and a luxurious interior with modern amenities. Visitors can witness this masterpiece alongside over 200 other classic vehicles, all at no cost.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum has recently expanded its prestigious Ron Sturgeon Collection with the addition of a 2002 Ferrari 575M Maranello, a vehicle that epitomizes the zenith of Italian sports car craftsmanship. This acquisition not only enriches the museum's diverse array of over 200 classic, exotic, and rare automobiles but also serves as a testament to Ferrari's enduring legacy in the automotive industry.

Equipped with a 5.7-liter quad-cam F133E V12 engine, the Ferrari 575M Maranello delivers an impressive 508 horsepower and 434 lb-ft of torque, complemented by a six-speed F1 sequential transaxle and a limited-slip differential for optimal performance. The car's Skyhook suspension system with adaptive dampers and yellow-finished Ferrari-branded calipers ensures a driving experience that is both exhilarating and secure.

Inside, the Ferrari 575M Maranello blends luxury with functionality, featuring automatic climate control, electric windows, and a Kenwood head unit with a remote CD changer. The driver-centric design includes a leather-wrapped three-spoke steering wheel with column-mounted shift paddles, alongside a 220-mph speedometer and a 7,600-rpm redline tachometer, underscoring the vehicle's performance-oriented ethos.

With only 15,000 miles on its odometer, this Ferrari represents a pristine example of automotive excellence. The DFW Car & Toy Museum, offering free admission, provides an invaluable opportunity for enthusiasts and the general public alike to engage with this piece of automotive history. Open Tuesday through Saturday, the museum continues to celebrate the innovation and artistry of vehicles like the Ferrari 575M Maranello, making it a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the evolution of automotive design and engineering.
