Rare 1980 Datsun 280ZX 10th Anniversary Edition Joins DFW Car & Toy Museum Collection

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The Ron Sturgeon Collection at the DFW Car & Toy Museum has proudly added a rare 1980 Datsun 280ZX 10th Anniversary Edition to its impressive lineup. This special edition, one of only 3,000 produced, features a unique Golden Mist and Thunder Black two-tone paint scheme, commemorative decals, and a host of premium features that celebrate the Z-car's decade-long legacy of innovation and style. Museum founder Ron Sturgeon highlights the car's significance as a turning point in Japanese sports car history, with its meticulously preserved details and performance features, including a fuel-injected 2.8L L28E inline-six engine and five-speed manual transmission.

Visitors to the DFW Car & Toy Museum can experience this golden icon alongside over 200 collectible vehicles and 3,000 vintage toys, all housed in a 150,000 square foot facility in North Fort Worth. The museum, founded by automotive enthusiast Ron Sturgeon, offers free admission and parking, making it a must-visit destination for car and toy lovers alike. The museum's website, DFW Elite Toy Museum, remains a valuable resource for enthusiasts worldwide, showcasing Sturgeon's passion for automobiles that began in his teenage years.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum in Fort Worth, Texas, has recently expanded its impressive collection with the acquisition of a 1980 Datsun 280ZX 10th Anniversary Edition, a vehicle that stands as a beacon of Japanese automotive innovation and style. This rare model, one of only 3,000 ever produced, is adorned with a unique Golden Mist and Thunder Black two-tone paint scheme, marking it as a significant piece of automotive history. The museum's founder, Ron Sturgeon, emphasizes the car's importance, not just as a vehicle but as a tribute to the legacy of the Z-car lineage.

The 10th Anniversary Edition of the Datsun 280ZX is renowned for its combination of performance and luxury. Equipped with a fuel-injected 2.8L L28E inline-six engine and a five-speed manual transmission, the car was ahead of its time in terms of technology and design. Its premium amenities, including tan leather seats, automatic climate control, and distinctive exterior details like commemorative decals and black window trim, highlight the model's special status. Features such as headlight washers and a rear window wiper further demonstrate the forward-thinking approach of its designers.

This addition to the DFW Car & Toy Museum not only enriches its collection but also serves as an educational tool for visitors, offering insights into the evolution of the automotive industry. The museum, which houses over 200 collectible vehicles and 3,000 vintage toys, provides free admission, making this piece of history accessible to everyone. The inclusion of the 1980 Datsun 280ZX 10th Anniversary Edition underscores the museum's commitment to preserving and showcasing the milestones of automotive design and engineering.

The significance of this acquisition extends beyond the museum's walls. It represents a moment in time when Japanese automakers began to assert their dominance in the global market, blending performance with luxury in ways that would influence the industry for decades to come. For enthusiasts, historians, and casual visitors alike, the car offers a tangible connection to an era of innovation and style that continues to resonate in today's automotive landscape.
