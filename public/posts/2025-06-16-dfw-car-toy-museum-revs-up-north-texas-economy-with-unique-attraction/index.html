DFW Car & Toy Museum Revs Up North Texas Economy with Unique Attraction

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The newly opened DFW Car & Toy Museum, located in a former Coors distribution facility, has become a must-see attraction in North Texas. At the recent Tarrant County Cars and Coffee event, the museum wowed visitors with over 200 cars on display, including rare and exotic models like Italian sports cars and British classics. The museum's unique exhibits, such as the 'Mystery Van' from Scooby-Doo, have garnered glowing reviews, with many calling it the best free museum in America.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum, nestled in the heart of North Texas, has rapidly ascended as a cultural and economic beacon, drawing over 2,000 visitors to its inaugural Cars and Coffee event. This 150,000-square-foot marvel, once a Coors distribution facility, now houses an eclectic collection of over 200 cars, ranging from rare Italian sports cars to the beloved 'Mystery Van' from Scooby-Doo, offering a nostalgic journey for visitors of all ages.

Ron Sturgeon, the museum's owner and a seasoned car collector, has meticulously curated a space that not only celebrates automotive history but also fosters community engagement. The museum's pet-friendly policy and free admission underscore its commitment to accessibility, making it a standout attraction in the region. Its strategic location near Meacham and I-35, coupled with ample parking, further enhances its appeal to a broad audience.

Beyond its role as a museum, the facility has emerged as a sought-after venue for private and corporate events, thanks to its climate-controlled environment and versatile spaces. This dual function not only amplifies its cultural impact but also bolsters the local economy by attracting visitors and events to North Fort Worth.

The museum's success story is a testament to Sturgeon's passion for automobiles, a journey that began in his teenage years. Today, his vision has materialized into a landmark that not only preserves automotive and toy heritage but also serves as a catalyst for economic growth and community building in Texas.

The DFW Car & Toy Museum's rise as a must-visit destination highlights the significant role of niche attractions in enriching the state's cultural and economic fabric. Its ability to draw crowds, host events, and offer free educational entertainment positions it as a model for how passion projects can evolve into impactful community assets.
