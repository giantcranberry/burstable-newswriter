NAFA Fleet Management Association Showcases Cutting-Edge Innovations at San Antonio Expo

Building Texas Show News • June 18, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

NAFA Fleet Management Association held a Media Day event at its annual Institute & Expo in San Antonio, TX. Over a dozen leading companies shared breaking news and innovations with industry reporters. The event showcased advancements in data analytics, safety technologies, electrification, and more.

Full Article

The NAFA Fleet Management Association's annual Institute & Expo in San Antonio became the epicenter of innovation for the fleet industry, as over a dozen companies unveiled groundbreaking technologies and solutions during Media Day. This event not only highlighted the industry's rapid evolution but also underscored its commitment to embracing digital transformation and sustainability.

Among the notable announcements, Draiver introduced an all-in-one Command Center for vehicle inventory management and a new Driver app featuring Vehicle Inspection and Self Assign technologies. These innovations aim to streamline operations and maximize uptime, reflecting the industry's move towards more connected and efficient fleet management solutions.

The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) shared insights from its engine repair survey, revealing a highly skilled workforce ready to meet the demands of modern fleet maintenance. This data is crucial for understanding the capabilities and certifications of technicians, ensuring fleets can rely on qualified professionals for their maintenance needs.

MoveEV's introduction of a marketplace add-in for the MyGeotab® Product represents a significant step forward for electric vehicle (EV) fleet management. By integrating home charging reimbursement data directly into the Geotab environment, MoveEV is simplifying the transition to EVs for fleets, making sustainable practices more accessible and manageable.

GreenPower Motor Company expanded its electric commercial vehicle lineup with the EV Star Utility Truck, designed for vocational applications. This addition not only diversifies the options available for businesses looking to electrify their fleets but also demonstrates the growing viability of EVs in various commercial sectors.

RoadFlex's Proactive Fuel Risk Management Platform™ offers fleets a novel approach to managing fuel costs, a critical factor in operational budgets. This platform exemplifies the industry's focus on leveraging technology to address traditional challenges, providing fleets with tools to navigate fluctuating fuel prices more effectively.

These announcements from NAFA's Media Day illustrate the fleet industry's dynamic response to the demands of modern commerce and environmental stewardship. By adopting advanced technologies and sustainable practices, the sector is setting a new standard for operational efficiency and ecological responsibility, with implications that extend far beyond Texas to the global economy.
