DFW Car & Toy Museum Enhances Collection with Historic 1971 Datsun 240Z Coupe

Building Texas Show News • June 15, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The DFW Car & Toy Museum, under the stewardship of renowned collector Ron Sturgeon, has added a historically significant 1971 Datsun 240Z Coupe to its impressive collection. This Series 1 model, beautifully restored by the Pierre Z Car Center in 1992, played a pivotal role in Nissan North America's Vintage Restoration Program of the 1990s. Featured in prestigious publications like Road & Track and Hemmings Sports & Exotic Car, this 240Z is not just a testament to automotive excellence but also a symbol of the preservation of Z-car heritage. Powered by a 2.4-liter L24 inline-six engine, it combines authenticity with reliability, boasting enhancements such as a tubular exhaust header and an electronic distributor conversion. The car's interior and exterior features, including factory instrumentation and distinctive Series 1 markers, make it a must-see for enthusiasts and historians alike.

Ron Sturgeon's passion for automobiles shines through in this acquisition, highlighting the 240Z's influence on the restoration and celebration of vintage Japanese sports cars. The DFW Car & Toy Museum, now located in North Fort Worth, offers free admission to over 200 classic vehicles and 3,000 vintage toys, making it a treasure trove for car and toy lovers. This addition underscores the museum's commitment to preserving automotive history and providing an engaging experience for visitors of all ages.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum's latest acquisition, a 1971 Datsun 240Z Coupe, represents more than just an addition to its impressive collection. This Series 1 model, meticulously restored in 1992, stands as a cornerstone in the history of automotive restoration, particularly for Japanese sports cars. Its role in the inception of Nissan North America's Vintage Restoration Program during the 1990s underscores its historical and cultural significance, marking a turning point in how vintage cars are preserved and celebrated.

Equipped with a 2.4-liter L24 inline-six engine, the 240Z boasts 151 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque, figures that speak to its performance legacy. The car's restoration included enhancements like a tubular exhaust header and an electronic distributor conversion, ensuring its reliability while preserving its authenticity. Features such as unique vents below the rear hatch glass and color-matched aftermarket mirrors, along with factory instrumentation, highlight the car's originality and the meticulous attention to detail in its restoration.

Ron Sturgeon, the founder of the DFW Car & Toy Museum, highlights the 240Z's impact on the appreciation and restoration of vintage Japanese sports cars. With only 34,000 miles on its odometer, the car is a pristine example of Z-car heritage, offering visitors a glimpse into a pivotal era of automotive design and engineering. The museum, which houses over 200 classic and collectible vehicles and more than 3,000 vintage toys, provides free admission, making this piece of automotive history accessible to all.

This acquisition not only enriches the museum's collection but also serves as a testament to the 240Z's enduring legacy in the automotive world. Its story is a reminder of the importance of preserving automotive history, offering insights into the evolution of car restoration practices and the cultural significance of classic cars. For enthusiasts and historians alike, the 240Z's presence in the DFW Car & Toy Museum is a celebration of automotive innovation and heritage.
