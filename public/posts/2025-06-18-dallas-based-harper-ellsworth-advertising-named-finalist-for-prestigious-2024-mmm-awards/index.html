Dallas-Based Harper Ellsworth Advertising Named Finalist for Prestigious 2024 MM+M Awards

Building Texas Show News • June 18, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Harper Ellsworth Advertising, with client Texas Radiotherapy, is a finalist for the 2024 MM+M Awards in the category of 'Use of Hospital or Healthcare Services Marketing'. The MM+M Awards event recognizes, champions, and celebrates creativity and effectiveness in healthcare marketing and communications. Finalists are invited to attend the awards gala in New York, New York on October 10, 2024. With an MM+M Award, recognition comes from industry peers. More than 90 judges boasting a wide variety of expertise narrowed down the hundreds of entries to a select few. It's this rigorous analysis that has made the MM+M Awards the industry's gold standard. Kathlene Simmons, CEO of Harper Ellsworth, stated, 'We are thrilled that our work for Texas Radiotherapy is selected as a finalist for such a prestigious award. This recognition is the result of incredible teamwork from many talented individuals. We all worked seamlessly together to help deliver the client's goals and make men more aware of prostate cancer.' Melvin Strobbe, Project Creative Director, added, 'The key to our creative output was having such a great client: Texas Radiotherapy. Dr. Matthew and Sara Cavey were excellent on and off camera and helped us hone a strategy, profiling actual patients, that was based on patient truth.'

Full Article

Harper Ellsworth Advertising, a Dallas-based full-service agency, has been recognized as a finalist for the 2024 MM+M Awards in the 'Use of Hospital or Healthcare Services Marketing' category for their collaborative campaign with Texas Radiotherapy. This nomination not only celebrates the agency's innovative approach to healthcare marketing but also underscores the significant role of strategic communication in enhancing public health awareness.

The MM+M Awards are revered in the healthcare marketing industry for honoring creativity and effectiveness, with a rigorous selection process involving over 90 judges. Harper Ellsworth's campaign, which focused on raising awareness about prostate cancer through patient-centric storytelling, stands out for its authenticity and impact. Kathlene Simmons, CEO of Harper Ellsworth, and Melvin Strobbe, Project Creative Director, both emphasized the collaborative effort and the importance of patient truth in their campaign's success.

This recognition is a testament to Harper Ellsworth's expertise in navigating the complexities of healthcare marketing to deliver messages that resonate with the public. Their work with Texas Radiotherapy exemplifies how creative marketing strategies can contribute to early detection and treatment of serious health conditions, ultimately saving lives. The nomination also highlights the growing diversity in the advertising industry, with Harper Ellsworth being a woman-owned business making significant strides in a competitive field.

The implications of this nomination extend beyond the accolades. It serves as a benchmark for the healthcare marketing industry, demonstrating the potential of well-crafted campaigns to influence public health outcomes positively. As the industry continues to evolve, the recognition of Harper Ellsworth's work by MM+M sets a high standard for creativity, effectiveness, and impact in healthcare communication.
