Texas Mineral Resources Corp. Chairman to Highlight Domestic Mineral Production at Harvard Business School Conference

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Texas Mineral Resources Corp. announces that Chairman Anthony Marchese will speak at the 2025 Technology and National Security Conference at Harvard Business School on April 5, 2025.

The focus is on energy, critical minerals, and domestic production amid global tensions, with TMRC owning an interest in the Round Top project in Texas.

Full Article

The 2025 Technology and National Security Conference, hosted by Harvard Business School, will feature Anthony Marchese, chairman of Texas Mineral Resources Corp., as a panelist. Scheduled for April 5, 2025, the panel titled 'Energy & Critical Minerals, Domestic Production in Great Power Competition' will delve into the challenges and strategies for securing U.S. energy and mineral needs amidst rising global tensions. Marchese's participation underscores the growing importance of domestic mineral production, particularly rare earth elements and technology metals, in ensuring national security and fostering technological innovation.

Texas Mineral Resources Corp.'s strategic focus on the Round Top project in Hudspeth County, Texas, where it holds a 19.3% interest, is a testament to the potential of domestic resources. The project aims to extract heavy rare earth elements, technology metals, and industrial minerals, which are pivotal for various high-tech applications. Marchese's insights at the conference are expected to shed light on how projects like Round Top can contribute to reducing the U.S.'s reliance on foreign mineral supplies, thereby enhancing economic and technological independence.

The timing of the conference is particularly significant, as global supply chains for critical minerals face increasing complexities and geopolitical sensitivities. Marchese's presentation will likely emphasize the strategic importance of developing domestic mineral resources to mitigate these challenges. By highlighting the Round Top project and similar initiatives, the panel discussion aims to foster a broader understanding of how domestic mineral production can support U.S. national security and technological leadership in an increasingly competitive global landscape.
