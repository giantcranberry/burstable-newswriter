OppFi's Stellar Q3 2024 Performance Signals Strong Growth in Financial Technology Sector

Building Texas Show News • June 18, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Stonegate Capital Partners Updates Coverage on OppFi (NYSE: OPFI), reporting impressive revenue, net income, and EPS for the quarter. The company's strong liquidity position and increased originations demonstrate a positive trajectory for growth. OPFI's ability to manage credit quality and raise guidance for FY24 further solidify its position in the market.

Full Article

OppFi, Inc., a leading financial technology company, has unveiled its third-quarter results for 2024, demonstrating exceptional growth and profitability. The company, known for its commitment to expanding credit access for underbanked populations, reported a revenue of $136.6 million, alongside an adjusted net income of $31.5 million and adjusted earnings per share of $0.36. These figures not only surpass the company's own projections but also exceed consensus estimates, marking a significant milestone in OppFi's journey.

The financial achievements of OppFi are underscored by a net revenue margin expansion of 992 basis points, reaching 66.7%. This remarkable growth is a testament to the company's strategic initiatives aimed at enhancing profitability and fostering sustainable expansion within its core operations. With $74.2 million in cash and restricted cash, and an additional $199.4 million in unused debt capacity, OppFi's liquidity position stands at a robust $273.6 million. This financial strength provides the company with ample flexibility to pursue future growth opportunities and potentially enhance shareholder value.

Lending activity during the quarter also saw a notable uptick, with total net originations amounting to $218.8 million. This represents an increase from both the previous quarter and the same period last year. A significant achievement was the improvement in the auto-approval rate to 77%, up from 72% in Q3 2023. All originations were facilitated through bank partners, highlighting the effectiveness of OppFi's partnership model. Furthermore, the company has made strides in credit quality, with charge-offs as a percentage of total revenue decreasing to 34.3%, down from 42% in the previous year, showcasing OppFi's adeptness at managing risk while expanding its loan portfolio.

In light of these strong results, OppFi has revised its full-year guidance upwards, now expecting adjusted net income between $74 million and $76 million, and adjusted EPS in the range of $0.85 to $0.87. This optimistic outlook reflects the company's resilience and strategic positioning in a challenging economic environment. As traditional financial institutions tighten lending criteria, OppFi's focus on serving underbanked consumers places it in a favorable position to capture additional market share and sustain its growth momentum.

The implications of OppFi's performance extend beyond the company itself, signaling a broader shift in the financial technology sector towards more inclusive lending practices. By addressing the needs of underbanked consumers, OppFi not only contributes to economic empowerment but also sets a benchmark for innovation and risk management in the industry. As economic uncertainties persist, OppFi's ability to maintain this balance will be critical to its continued success and influence in the evolving financial landscape.
