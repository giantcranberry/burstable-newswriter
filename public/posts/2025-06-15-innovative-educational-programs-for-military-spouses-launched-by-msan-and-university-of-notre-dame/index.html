Innovative Educational Programs for Military Spouses Launched by MSAN and University of Notre Dame

Building Texas Show News • June 15, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The Military Spouse Advocacy Network (MSAN) and the Leadership Institute for Military Spouses (LIMS) have partnered with the University of Notre Dame to offer free training programs for military spouses. These programs focus on ethical leadership skills and mental health wellness, aiming to empower military spouses to become community leaders. The courses are developed by faculty from various Notre Dame centers and will be delivered online.

Full Article

The Military Spouse Advocacy Network (MSAN) and the University of Notre Dame have joined forces to launch two groundbreaking educational programs tailored for military spouses. These initiatives, focusing on leadership in ethics and mental health wellness, are set to empower participants with essential skills and knowledge to navigate the complexities of military life. The programs are a testament to the commitment of both organizations to support military families through education and professional development.

Designed exclusively for alumni of the Leadership Institute for Military Spouses (LIMS) Leadership Development Program, the courses will welcome cohorts of 50 students each. The Leadership in Ethics course aims to hone skills in conflict management, workplace communication, and ethical decision-making. Meanwhile, the Mental Health Wellness course addresses the unique mental health challenges faced by military families, offering strategies for resilience and well-being.

This collaboration underscores the importance of providing military spouses with the tools they need to thrive both personally and professionally. By focusing on leadership and mental health, the programs not only benefit the participants but also have the potential to positively impact their communities and the broader military family network. The involvement of faculty and staff from multiple Notre Dame centers and clinics highlights the multidisciplinary approach taken to develop these programs, ensuring a comprehensive and impactful learning experience.

The initiative reflects a growing recognition of the sacrifices made by military families and the need for targeted support to address their unique challenges. Through these programs, MSAN and the University of Notre Dame are setting a precedent for how educational institutions and advocacy groups can collaborate to make a meaningful difference in the lives of those who serve our country.
