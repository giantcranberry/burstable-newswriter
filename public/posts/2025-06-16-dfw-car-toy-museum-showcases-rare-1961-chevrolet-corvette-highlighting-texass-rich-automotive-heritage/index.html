DFW Car & Toy Museum Showcases Rare 1961 Chevrolet Corvette, Highlighting Texas's Rich Automotive Heritage

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The DFW Car & Toy Museum in Fort Worth, TX, unveils the 1961 Chevrolet Corvette 283 315 Fuelie 4-Speed, a rare gem from the Ron Sturgeon Collection. This iconic Corvette boasts a powerful 283ci V8 engine and a captivating light blue exterior, representing the pinnacle of early Corvette performance.

The meticulously preserved '61 Corvette showcases precision engineering and classic style, with a cockpit designed for high-speed adventures. With only 51,000 miles on the odometer, this vintage beauty remains a must-see for car enthusiasts at the DFW Car & Toy Museum.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum has recently added a significant piece to its collection, a light blue 1961 Chevrolet Corvette 283 315 Fuelie 4-Speed, showcasing the pinnacle of early 1960s American automotive engineering. This vehicle, part of the Ron Sturgeon Collection, is not just a car but a testament to the innovation and performance that defined an era. With its factory-equipped fuel-injected 283-cubic-inch V8 engine, producing 315 horsepower, and a four-speed manual transmission, the Corvette represents a milestone in automotive design and technology.

What makes this exhibit particularly noteworthy is the Corvette's exceptional condition, with only 51,000 miles on its odometer. The interior, featuring a three-spoke steering wheel and a suite of gauges including a 160-mph speedometer and a tachometer with a 6,600-rpm redline, offers visitors a rare look at the craftsmanship of the time. The Rochester mechanical fuel injection system and high-lift camshaft further highlight the vehicle's performance capabilities, making it a must-see for classic car enthusiasts and historians alike.

The inclusion of this Corvette in the DFW Car & Toy Museum's collection is more than just an addition; it's a celebration of Texas's contribution to preserving and celebrating automotive history. Located in North Fort Worth, the museum's 150,000-square-foot facility provides free admission, making this piece of history accessible to all. This exhibit not only serves as a bridge to the past but also educates future generations about the significance of American muscle cars in shaping automotive culture.

For the local community and visitors, the museum offers a unique opportunity to connect with an important part of American heritage. The display of the 1961 Chevrolet Corvette underscores the museum's commitment to showcasing vehicles that have made a lasting impact on the automotive industry and culture. It's a reminder of how far automotive technology has come and the timeless appeal of classic cars.
