1978 Datsun Pickup Joins DFW Car & Toy Museum's Ron Sturgeon Collection

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The DFW Car & Toy Museum has recently added a 1978 Datsun Pickup to its renowned Ron Sturgeon Collection, showcasing a vehicle that epitomizes the durability and charm of late '70s automotive design. This classic truck, powered by a 2.0-liter inline-four engine and featuring a five-speed manual transmission, offers an analog driving experience that's rare in today's market. Museum founder Ron Sturgeon praises the pickup for its no-frills reliability and the patina that tells its story of hard work. Inside, the truck maintains its original simplicity with a stock AM radio and manual heater, appealing to vintage truck enthusiasts who value authenticity over luxury.

The DFW Car & Toy Museum, now located in North Fort Worth, spans 150,000 square feet and houses over 200 collector vehicles and 3,000 nostalgic toys. Founded by Ron Sturgeon, a lifelong automotive enthusiast, the museum offers free admission and a unique glimpse into automotive history. The museum's new facility also provides climate-controlled car storage and event spaces, making it a hub for car lovers and collectors alike. Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum has recently expanded its Ron Sturgeon Collection with the addition of a 1978 Datsun Pickup, a vehicle that stands as a testament to the rugged reliability and simplicity of late 1970s automotive design. This pickup, powered by a 2.0-liter inline-four engine and equipped with a five-speed manual transmission, offers visitors a stark contrast to the technology-driven vehicles of today, emphasizing an analog driving experience that harks back to a simpler time in automotive history.

Ron Sturgeon, the museum's founder, emphasizes the pickup's significance, pointing to its durability and utility as key characteristics that define its appeal. The vehicle's interior, featuring a stock AM radio and manual heater, along with visible wear on the driver-side seat, serves as a tangible reminder of its hardworking past. These elements not only enhance the museum's diverse collection but also provide a unique attraction for enthusiasts of vintage trucks and automotive history.

Located in North Fort Worth, the DFW Car & Toy Museum is home to over 200 vehicles and 3,000 toys within a 150,000-square-foot facility. The museum's acquisition of the 1978 Datsun Pickup underscores its commitment to preserving and celebrating the rich heritage of the automotive industry. By offering free admission, the museum ensures that this piece of history, along with its entire collection, is accessible to all who wish to explore the evolution of automotive design and technology.

The inclusion of the Datsun Pickup in the museum's collection is more than just an addition of another vehicle; it's a celebration of automotive history and a nod to the enduring appeal of vehicles built for durability and functionality. For visitors, it offers a rare opportunity to connect with the past and appreciate the advancements in automotive technology, making it a significant addition to the museum's already impressive lineup.
