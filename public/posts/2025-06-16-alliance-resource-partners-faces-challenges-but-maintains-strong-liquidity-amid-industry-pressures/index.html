Alliance Resource Partners Faces Challenges but Maintains Strong Liquidity Amid Industry Pressures

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Alliance Resource Partners, L.P. (ARLP) experienced a challenging 4Q24 with lower coal sales pricing and higher operating expenses, resulting in a 5.6% decrease in total revenues. Despite this, ARLP remains committed to its FY25 guidance, anticipating improvements through operational efficiency and a growing order book. Stonegate Capital Partners provided updates on ARLP's financial performance, reporting revenue, adj EBITDA, and adj EPS figures for the quarter.

Full Article

Alliance Resource Partners, L.P. (NASDAQ: ARLP) has recently disclosed its fourth-quarter results for 2024, revealing a 5.6% year-over-year decline in total revenues to $590.1 million. This performance downturn is attributed to a combination of lower coal sales pricing and heightened operating expenses, underscoring the broader pressures facing the industry. Notably, the company's net income experienced a sharp decrease to $16.3 million from $115.4 million in the same quarter of the previous year, partly due to non-cash impairment charges related to its MC Mining operation.

Despite these challenges, Alliance Resource Partners showcases a robust liquidity position, with $593.9 million available, including $137.0 million in cash and $456.9 million in accessible credit. The company also reported generating $75.2 million in free cash flow for the quarter, contributing to a year-to-date total of $383.5 million. This financial stability is a testament to the company's resilience in a fluctuating market.

The coal operations segment saw a 3.3% drop in sales revenue to $504.6 million, with coal sales volumes decreasing by 2.3% to 8.4 million tons. However, the Illinois Basin segment demonstrated some resilience with a 2.8% increase in sales volumes, contrasting with the Appalachia segment's 17.1% decline due to reduced production at Tunnel Ridge. The royalty business segment also faced difficulties, with total royalty revenues falling 8.6% to $48.5 million, despite a slight increase in oil and gas royalty volumes.

Looking forward, Alliance Resource Partners is optimistic about its fiscal year 2025 guidance, expecting improvements from operational efficiencies, a strengthening order book, and declining domestic inventories. The company's decision to maintain its quarterly cash distribution at $0.70 per unit reflects confidence in its financial health and future prospects. Stonegate Capital Partners' analysis suggests a valuation range of $27.89 to $30.97 per unit, indicating potential growth in the evolving energy market landscape.

This news is significant for stakeholders and the industry at large, as it highlights both the current challenges and the potential for recovery and growth in the energy sector. Alliance Resource Partners' ability to maintain a strong liquidity position and generate substantial free cash flow amidst industry-wide pressures underscores its strategic positioning for future opportunities.
