OYO's Surge in Summer Bookings Highlights Texas as a Key Market

Building Texas Show News • June 18, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Global hospitality technology company OYO reported a 16% increase in summer booking revenue (June to August) in the US compared to last year, according to STR, significantly outperforming the broader economy hotel segment that experienced a 2% decline, underlining its growing footprint in the world’s largest hotel market. The growth was primarily driven by a 15% increase in bookings across OYO hotels in the US. The company saw particularly robust performance in the Midwest and Northeast regions, recording 46% and 44% growth in booking revenue respectively, substantially outpacing the market growth of 22% and 24%.

Full Article

OYO's recent announcement of a 16% increase in summer booking revenue from June to August, compared to the previous year, marks a significant milestone for the hospitality industry in Texas and beyond. This growth is particularly noteworthy as it contrasts with a 2% decline in the broader economy hotel segment, according to STR data. Texas emerged as the top-performing state for OYO, contributing 22% of the company's overall summer booking revenue, with Houston playing a pivotal role by accounting for 5% of the total.

The company's success is attributed to its technology-driven platform, which offers seamless bookings, dynamic pricing, and AI-powered customer support, among other features. Nikhil Heda, Head of Business Development for OYO US, highlighted the company's focus on quality accommodations at competitive prices as a key factor in its performance. This strategy has not only allowed OYO to outperform market averages in regions like the Midwest and Northeast but also to set a new standard in the economy hotel segment.

OYO's expansion plans, including the acquisition of G6 Hospitality and the addition of approximately 250 more hotels to its US portfolio in 2024, signal its ambition to further solidify its presence in the market. The company's ability to thrive in a challenging economic environment and post-pandemic travel landscape underscores the effectiveness of its approach. For travelers, this means greater access to affordable and quality accommodations, while for the industry, OYO's success serves as a compelling example of how technology and data-driven strategies can drive growth.

The implications of OYO's performance extend beyond its own growth. It challenges other players in the hospitality sector to innovate and adapt to changing consumer preferences and market conditions. As OYO continues to expand, its impact on the competitive landscape and travel patterns in the US will be closely watched by industry observers and stakeholders alike.
