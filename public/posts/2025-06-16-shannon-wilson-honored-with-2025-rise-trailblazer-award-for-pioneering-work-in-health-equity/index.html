Shannon Wilson Honored with 2025 RISE Trailblazer Award for Pioneering Work in Health Equity

Building Texas Show News • June 16, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The RISE Trailblazer Award, formerly known as the Dr. Martin L. Block Award for Innovation & Excellence, was presented to Shannon Wilson at RISE National for her significant contributions to improving the healthcare system and advocating for health equity. Wilson, appointed as Priority Health's first vice president of population health and equity, was chosen as the winner out of seven finalists by the RISE community. Her dedication to enhancing the well-being of vulnerable populations and her leadership in various industry and community boards have set her apart.

Full Article

Shannon Wilson's recognition with the 2025 RISE Trailblazer Award highlights her significant role in transforming senior healthcare and her dedication to health equity. As vice president of population health and equity at Priority Health, Wilson has been instrumental in developing strategies that aim to reduce healthcare disparities and improve outcomes for vulnerable populations. Her work spans across Medicare, Medicaid, commercial, and individual business lines, showcasing her broad impact on the healthcare industry.

The award, voted on by the RISE community, celebrates individuals who demonstrate exceptional commitment to healthcare innovation. Wilson's ability to understand the intersection between health and race has led to the creation of initiatives that not only enhance healthcare experiences but also foster more inclusive healthcare systems. Her leadership extends beyond her professional role, as she actively contributes to community health and social services through her involvement with various boards.

Wilson's achievements underscore the critical need for healthcare systems to address disparities and work towards equity. Her recognition serves as a reminder of the importance of innovative thinking and compassionate leadership in creating healthcare solutions that benefit all segments of the population. The implications of her work are far-reaching, offering a model for how healthcare professionals can contribute to systemic change and improve the lives of those they serve.
