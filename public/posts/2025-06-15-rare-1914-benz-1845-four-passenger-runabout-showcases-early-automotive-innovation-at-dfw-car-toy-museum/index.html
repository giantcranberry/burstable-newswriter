Rare 1914 Benz 18/45 Four Passenger Runabout Showcases Early Automotive Innovation at DFW Car & Toy Museum

Building Texas Show News • June 15, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

The DFW Car & Toy Museum showcases the rare 1914 Benz 18/45 Four Passenger Runabout from the Ron Sturgeon Collection, highlighting the luxury motoring era and Karl Benz's engineering brilliance. With a $3,250 price tag in 1911, this model catered to the elite, boasting superior performance and design. Powered by a 45 horsepower engine, the Benz 18/45 features iconic aesthetics and a racing pedigree, setting land speed records and embodying automotive innovation.

Full Article

The DFW Car & Toy Museum has become the temporary home to a rare 1914 Benz 18/45 Four Passenger Runabout, a vehicle that encapsulates the innovation and luxury of early automotive engineering. This exhibit not only serves as a window into the past but also underscores the museum's commitment to preserving the rich history of the automobile industry.

Manufactured during a time when the automotive industry was undergoing significant transformation, the Benz 18/45 was a symbol of status and technological advancement. With its 4.9-liter four-cylinder L-Head engine producing 45 horsepower, the vehicle was a marvel of its time, designed for the elite who valued performance and elegance. Its distinctive features, such as the V-shaped radiator and unique bodywork, set it apart from the mass-produced models of the era, showcasing Benz's dedication to craftsmanship and innovation.

The Benz 18/45's connection to the brand's racing heritage, particularly the Blitzen Benz's land speed records, adds a layer of historical significance to the exhibit. This vehicle is not just a static display but a testament to the pioneering spirit that drove the automotive industry forward in the early 20th century.

Part of the Ron Sturgeon Collection, the 1914 Benz 18/45 is among over 200 extraordinary automobiles housed at the DFW Car & Toy Museum. The museum's free admission and dog-friendly policy make it an accessible venue for automotive enthusiasts and families alike to explore the evolution of automotive design and technology.

By showcasing vehicles like the 1914 Benz 18/45, the DFW Car & Toy Museum plays a crucial role in educating the public about the automotive industry's history and its impact on modern transportation. This exhibit not only celebrates the ingenuity of early automotive pioneers but also inspires future generations to appreciate and continue the legacy of innovation in the field.
