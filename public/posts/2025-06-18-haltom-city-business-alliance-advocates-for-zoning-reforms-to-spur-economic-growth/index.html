Haltom City Business Alliance Advocates for Zoning Reforms to Spur Economic Growth

Building Texas Show News • June 18, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

A recent article by the nonprofit group Strong Towns emphasizes the importance of taking immediate action to address affordable housing and attract new businesses in cities. The article highlights the need for simple, high-impact reforms to zoning codes to stimulate incremental growth. Cities like Dubuque, Iowa, have successfully reformed zoning laws to encourage development, while organizations like the Haltom United Business Alliance in Haltom City advocate for similar changes to eliminate unnecessary parking mandates hindering housing development and business investment.

Full Article

The Haltom United Business Alliance (HUBA) is making a compelling case for Haltom City to embrace zoning code reforms as a catalyst for economic revitalization. At the heart of their advocacy is the call to eliminate outdated parking mandates that they argue are stifling growth by deterring potential investors and developers. This initiative, inspired by successful examples in other cities, seeks to address the city's rising commercial vacancy rates and urban challenges through strategic, low-cost adjustments to local zoning laws.

HUBA's push for reform is grounded in the belief that small, incremental changes can have a significant impact. By focusing on parking requirements, which often pose a barrier to development, the alliance aims to create a more flexible regulatory environment. This approach not only promises to attract small businesses and facilitate housing development but also aligns with the evolving needs of modern retail and commercial landscapes. The potential benefits extend beyond immediate economic gains, offering a pathway to enhanced community resilience and a stronger local economy.

The situation in Haltom City mirrors broader urban challenges faced by municipalities across the country. With declining business occupancy and increasing homelessness, the need for innovative solutions has never been more pressing. HUBA's recommendations draw on the success of cities like Dubuque, Iowa, where nuanced zoning reforms have unlocked new development opportunities. By adopting similar strategies, Haltom City could position itself as a leader in proactive municipal planning, setting a precedent for others to follow.

The implications of HUBA's advocacy are far-reaching. For local businesses, the proposed reforms could mean easier access to prime locations and reduced operational hurdles. For residents, the potential increase in housing development could alleviate some of the pressures contributing to homelessness. On a broader scale, Haltom City's willingness to adapt its zoning laws could serve as a model for other communities grappling with similar challenges, demonstrating the power of strategic planning in fostering economic growth and community well-being.
