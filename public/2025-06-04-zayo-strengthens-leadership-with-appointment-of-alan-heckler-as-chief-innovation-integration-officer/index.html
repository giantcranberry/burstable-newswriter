Zayo Strengthens Leadership with Appointment of Alan Heckler as Chief Innovation & Integration Officer

Newswriter Business and Technology News • June 4, 2025
Read Original Article →

Summary

Zayo, a global leader in communications infrastructure, has announced the appointment of Alan Heckler as its new Chief Innovation & Integration Officer. With over 25 years of experience in digital infrastructure and M&A strategy, Heckler is set to spearhead the integration of Zayo’s recent acquisition of Crown Castle’s fiber solutions business and drive transformation initiatives aimed at accelerating the company's long-term growth. This move underscores Zayo's commitment to enhancing its network to meet the burgeoning demands of AI, cloud, and next-generation technologies. CEO Steve Smith highlighted Heckler's role in scaling the business and delivering greater value to customers, reinforcing Zayo's position in the digital infrastructure ecosystem. Heckler, previously a Senior Partner at Kearney, brings a wealth of experience in large-scale transformations across various industries, promising to align Zayo's operations and capabilities for sustained growth and innovation.

Zayo's strategic expansions, including a $20 billion investment in infrastructure over the past five years and the addition of nearly three million fiber miles, demonstrate its dedication to supporting the digital economy's evolution. Heckler's appointment comes at a pivotal time as Zayo continues to expand its network, notably through the Crown Castle acquisition and significant long-haul investments to bolster AI capabilities. His focus will be on ensuring seamless integration of new assets and aligning strategy, operations, and technology to foster innovation and meet customer needs in the rapidly advancing tech landscape.

Full Article

Zayo, a global communications infrastructure leader, has taken a significant step forward in its growth strategy by appointing Alan Heckler as its new Chief Innovation & Integration Officer. This move highlights Zayo's dedication to enhancing its digital infrastructure ecosystem through strategic leadership and acquisitions, including the recent purchase of Crown Castle’s fiber solutions business. Heckler's extensive 25-year background in digital infrastructure, industrial transformation, and M&A strategy positions him as a key figure in driving Zayo's integration efforts and transformation initiatives.

Steve Smith, CEO of Zayo, emphasized the importance of Heckler's role in scaling the company's operations and delivering greater value to customers. Zayo's aggressive expansion, marked by over $20 billion invested in infrastructure growth over the past five years and the addition of nearly three million fiber miles to its network, underscores its commitment to providing high-speed, reliable connectivity solutions. Heckler's appointment is a clear indicator of Zayo's ambition to lead the digital infrastructure sector, especially as the demand for AI, cloud, and next-generation technologies continues to rise.

Heckler shared his excitement about joining Zayo during such a critical phase of its growth. He pointed to the Crown Castle acquisition as a unique opportunity to strategically expand Zayo's network and strengthen its infrastructure in key areas. Before his role at Zayo, Heckler's leadership at Kearney saw the Americas Industrial Product Practice double in size, demonstrating his capability to foster significant growth and transformation. His expertise is expected to be instrumental in seamlessly integrating new acquisitions and driving innovation that aligns with Zayo's long-term objectives.

This strategic appointment not only reinforces Zayo's position as a leader in the digital infrastructure space but also signals its readiness to support the next wave of technological advancements. With Heckler at the helm of innovation and integration, Zayo is well-positioned to meet the evolving needs of its customers and the industry at large, ensuring its continued growth and success in a rapidly changing technological landscape.
